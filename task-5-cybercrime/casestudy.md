UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into authorizing fraudulent transactions. It usually happens through phishing links, fake payment requests, or social engineering tactics.

In a typical scenario, the attacker contacts the victim through a phone call, message, or social media, pretending to be a bank official, seller, or customer. They may send a fake payment link or request and ask the victim to enter their UPI PIN to “receive money.” However, entering the PIN actually authorizes a payment from the victim’s account instead of receiving money.

Students and young users are often targeted because they frequently use UPI apps like Google Pay or PhonePe and may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences of such fraud include financial loss, stress, and compromised personal data.

This type of cybercrime highlights the importance of awareness and caution while using digital payment platforms.
